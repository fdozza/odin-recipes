Chili

Description

Want a delectable warm stew during the coming winter months? Look no further as this chili will warm your bones and potentially burn your tongue as it packs some serious heat via the key ingredient: habanero peppers. Follow along and get yourself right this winter.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 lb. carrots, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 stalk of celery, chopped
  • 3 cans of San Marzano crushed tomatoes
  • 1/3 cup tomato paste
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 lb. Beef chuck, patted dry
  • 1 lb. Ground Pork
  • 3 Tbsp. Lea & Perrin's sauce
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 Tbsp. Grapeseed Oil
  • 1 Tbsp. Cumin
  • 1 Tbsp. Garlic Powder
  • 1 Tbsp. Onion Powder
  • 1 Tbsp. Black pepper
  • 3 habanero peppers
  • 1 cup chives
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 cups water

Steps

  1. Chop up all the vegetables and set them aside.
  2. Grab a dutch oven or any pot with a sturdy bottom and fill with grapeseed oil.
  3. Set the pot to high heat and dump in the beef chuck. Ensure the meat is evenly distributed as to not steam the meat.
  4. Remove the beef chuck once browned and add in the ground pork, following the same cooking instructions as the beef chuck.
  5. Once browned, remove the ground pork and use the fat found in the pot to saute the vegetables set aside earlier.
  6. Drop in the tomato paste and mix well with the vegetables
  7. Next, mix in the cooked beef chuck and ground pork and cook on high.
  8. Season the meat and vegetables with the above reference ingredients.
  9. Dump in the can of San Marzano tomatoes and two cups of water and stir the chili.
  10. Let chili come to a boil and reduece the heat to low and let cook for 3 hours, stirring every 30 minutes and doing a seasoning dump every hour.
  11. Serve with chives and sour cream.
